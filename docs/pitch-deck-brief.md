# GKS-for-U Pitch Deck Brief — Evidence-Based

## TARGET USER (ICP)

**18–24 year-old students from developing countries applying to GKS (Global Korea Scholarship) Undergraduate program.**

- 69 countries eligible, 367 seats/year (2025 cycle)
- Estimated **10,000–15,000 applicants/year** → acceptance rate **5–13%**
- Most are non-English speakers, zero Korean ability, no study-abroad experience
- Cumulative **104,000+ GKS scholars** funded since program expansion

## PROBLEM 1: Eligibility Judgment Failure (Urgent)

Applicants must self-assess against a **33-page English PDF** with **9 hard disqualifiers** (age, citizenship, prior degree, GPA across 5 scales, etc.). Many prepare for months only to discover they never qualified.

**Evidence:**
- GPA cutoff is strict: 79.5% **cannot be rounded up** to 80% → automatic rejection. (Source: Filipina Sa Korea — https://filipinasakorea.wordpress.com/2021/01/28/gks-faqs-grades-gpa-cgpa-qualifications-and-conversion/)
- 5 different GPA scales (4.0, 4.3, 4.5, 5.0, 100-point). Students whose universities use non-standard scales must get **official conversion certificates** from their institution. Many universities don't know how to provide this. (Source: TOPIK Guide — https://www.topikguide.com/global-korea-scholarship-gks-official-gpa-conversion-table/)
- The scholarship was renamed from KGSP → GKS in 2010, and both names still circulate, causing search confusion for first-time applicants.

---

## PROBLEM 2: Track & University Selection Trap (Large)

Choosing the wrong track or university = immediate disqualification. Rules are buried in the guideline PDF.

**Evidence:**
- Embassy Track: choose up to 3 universities, but **must include at least 1 Type B** (regional). Miss this rule → rejected.
- University Track: **1 university, 1 department only**. Wrong pick = no second chance.
- Applying to **both tracks in the same year = automatic disqualification**.
- Dedicated blog posts and Facebook threads exist solely to explain Embassy vs University track differences → proof that this is a recurring pain point. (Source: GKSScholarships.com — https://gksscholarships.com/gks-embassy-track-vs-university-track/ / StudyInKorea360 — https://studyinkorea360.com/how-to-apply/embassy-vs-university-track/)

---

## PROBLEM 3: Document Preparation Dropout (Underserved)

The application requires 10+ documents from multiple institutions, with apostille/notarization chains. No guided tooling exists.

**Evidence:**

> *"This process is expensive both in terms of cost and patience, and there will be moments when you feel lost, frustrated, or even doubt yourself."*
> — LKI School of Korean Language (https://learnkorean.in/gks-documents-submission-challenges-you-might-face-from-one-applicant-to-another/)

- One applicant waited **7–8 months** for a final degree certificate.
- Essay writing alone took **~3 months**.
- The study plan is the most important document but least understood:

> *"Most GKS applicants spend weeks perfecting their personal statement and completely overlook the document that Korean professors actually read first — the study plan."*
> — Scholars Academie (https://scholarsacademie.com/blog/gks/gks-study-plan-tips/)

- Apostille requirements are complex enough that a **dedicated service** (apostille.in) exists just for GKS applicants. (Source: https://apostille.in/gks-global-korea-scholarship/)

## WHY AI — "Product couldn't exist without AI"

| Feature | Without AI | With AI |
|---|---|---|
| Eligibility check | Read 33-page PDF yourself | Answer 6 questions → instant result |
| GPA conversion | Manual calculation, error-prone | Auto-convert across 5 scales |
| Essay feedback | Pay $300+ consultant or guess | Claude-powered rubric scoring + line-by-line suggestions, unlimited |
| University matching | Browse 68 universities blindly | Personalized recommendation based on GPA, country quota, field |

The AI essay feedback is the core differentiator — it uses the **actual GKS evaluation rubric** (motivation, academic preparation, post-graduation plan, character, clarity) to score and give specific, actionable feedback. No human consultant can do this at scale for free.
